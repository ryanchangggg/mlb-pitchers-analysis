This suggests Ohtani's stuff may be *easier to recognize* or less deceptive than Sanchez's, allowing batters to better distinguish balls from strikes.

## 5. Run Expectancy Impact

The run expectancy delta (delta_pitcher_run_exp) measures the change in expected runs following each pitch. This is perhaps the most direct measure of a pitcher's contribution to run prevention.

|---|---|---|---|---|
| Ohtani | **+21.028** | **+0.0158** | 528 | 717 |
| Sanchez | **+14.487** | **+0.0080** | 702 | 998 |
| Misiorowski | **+36.807** | **+0.0222** | 589 | 963 |

**Sanchez is the clear leader here.** His total run expectancy impact (+14.487) is the lowest (best) among the three, despite facing substantially more batters (1,800 pitches vs 1,335). On a per-pitch basis, Sanchez (+0.0080) gives up less than half the run value of Ohtani (+0.0158). This is a decisive finding: Sanchez has been significantly more effective at preventing runs than Ohtani on a per-pitch basis.

Misiorowski's numbers look elevated here, which is somewhat surprising given his elite strikeout rates. This may reflect the high-variance nature of his approach (strikeout or walk/home run) and the impact of his 9 home runs allowed in high-leverage situations.

## 7. Overall Assessment

### Evidence Supporting the Thesis

The data reveals several concrete areas where Ohtani's pitching is outperformed by his peers:

1. **Control Deficit:** Ohtani walks batters at a 58% higher rate than Sanchez (7.6% vs 4.8%). This is a significant disadvantage that reflects worse command.

2. **Run Prevention:** Sanchez gives up nearly half the run expectancy per pitch (+0.0080) compared to Ohtani (+0.0158). Despite fewer total pitches, Ohtani's total run cost (+21.0) exceeds Sanchez's (+14.5).

3. **Swing-and-Miss:** Ohtani's overall whiff rate (28.4%) trails both Sanchez (29.7%) and Misiorowski (34.3%). Sanchez's changeup (42.0%) and slider (42.7%) generate whiff rates that are genuinely elite.

4. **Durability:** Ohtani's reduced workload (14 games, 1,335 pitches vs 19 games, 1,800 for Sanchez) limits his overall value.

5. **Deception Gap:** Despite throwing more pitches in the zone (50.3% Zone%), batters chase Ohtani less (29.8% Chase%) than Sanchez (38.2%), suggesting his pitches are easier to identify.

### Counterpoints and Nuance

Ohtani does demonstrate genuine strengths: suppressing exit velocity (86.9 mph avg), zero barrels allowed, and lower Hard Hit% than Sanchez. His expected stats (xwOBA 0.257) are better than Sanchez's (0.279), and his overall batting line allowed (.162/.238/.235) is strong.

However, these advantages do not translate into better actual run prevention. The run expectancy data is unequivocal: Sanchez prevents runs more efficiently per pitch. In the context of the Cy Young race, where run prevention and innings are paramount, Sanchez holds the edge over Ohtani.

### Final Verdict

**Cristopher Sanchez** has been the superior pitcher to Shohei Ohtani in 2026. His elite command (4.8% BB%), devastating sinker-changeup combination, extreme ground ball tendencies (192 GB), and efficient run prevention (+0.0080 RE per pitch) make a compelling case that his pitching performance exceeds Ohtani's.

**Jacob Misiorowski** is arguably the most dominant of the three in terms of raw stuff with a 39.6% strikeout rate, 34.3% whiff rate, and 54.6% strike rate. His per-pitch dominance is unmatched, though his approach carries more variance.

Ohtani remains an elite pitcher, but the data does not support the claim that he is the *best* pitcher among these three NL aces in 2026. On the contrary, a thorough analysis of the pitch-level data demonstrates that both Sanchez and Misiorowski have outperformed him in several critical areas that matter most for pitcher evaluation.
